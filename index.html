Essential Travel Tips

Navigation

Download Naver Map or KakaoMap. Google Maps lacks walking directions in Korea.

T-Money Card

Buy a transit card at any convenience store (cash only). Use it for subways, buses, and taxis.

Currency (KRW)

Cards are universally accepted, but keep ₩20k-₩50k in cash for street food and T-Money reloads.

Connectivity

Type C/F plugs (220V). E-SIMs (like Airalo or Nomad) are highly recommended for seamless data.

Palaces & Traditional Streets

Description

Embrace the quiet grandeur of the past. Start your morning by renting a traditional Hanbok and wandering through the sweeping courtyards of Gyeongbokgung Palace. In the afternoon, lose yourself in the winding, tea-house-filled alleyways of Bukchon Hanok Village. End your day exploring hidden retro cafes in Ikseon-dong, capped with a sizzling authentic Korean BBQ dinner.

K-Culture & Urban Rhythm

Description

Find a moment of awe among the towering floor-to-ceiling bookshelves at Starfield Library in the bustling heart of Gangnam. In the afternoon, head to Hongdae to experience the raw energy of modern youth culture, boutique shopping, vibrant street food, and thrilling live busking performances in the evening.

City Views & Market Life

Description

Rise above the city by riding the scenic Namsan Cable Car to the iconic N Seoul Tower observation deck. Later, feast on Kalguksu (knife-cut noodles) in the bustling alleys of Namdaemun Market, and find peace with a serene evening stroll along the beautifully illuminated Cheonggyecheon Stream.

Industrial Chic & Speakeasies

Description

Discover experimental boutiques and sip artisan coffee in Seongsu-dong, often called the "Brooklyn of Seoul". In the evening, navigate the unassuming, gritty back alleys of Hipjiro (Euljiro) to uncover some of the city's most trendy hidden bars and vintage speakeasies.

Skyline, History & Farewell

Description

Wrap up your journey with breathtaking views from Lotte World Tower and riverside Ramyeon at Hangang Park. Explore profound history with a day trip to the DMZ, unwind completely at a traditional Jimjilbang bathhouse, and celebrate your final night in Myeong-dong with a shopping haul and classic Korean Fried Chicken and Beer (Chimaek).
